plastic types

The facility would have the potential to reprocess the following plastic types:
  • - Polyethylene terephthalate (PET)
  • - High-density polyethylene (HDPE)
  • - Low-density polyethylene (LDPE)
  • - Polyvinyl chloride (PVC)
  • - Polypropylene (PP) and Polystyrene (PS)

The project includes the construction and operation of a waste plastics sorting and plastics recycling facility with operations across two buildings.
In the first building, the facility would receive mixed plastics, remove unwanted materials such as glass pieces and caps and other recyclables, sterilise and deodorise the material using a pure natural, patented disinfectant solution, and sort the material by plastic type using optical, smart arm methods. Also located within the first building would be a centre for recycling research and product development. The pellets and flakes would be suitable for sale at this point or they could be transported to the second building to produce more advanced plastic products such as polyester fibre and resins.
In the second building, the facility would convert the plastic pellets or flakes into polyester fibres and other plastic products, using leading edge, high technology processes.
